Health & Social Media

These days, most of us use social media platforms like Facebook, Snapchat, YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram to stay connected

While it's great for keeping in touch, social media can also bring serious mental health issues if we don't manage it well.

By recognizing these problems and changing how we use social media, we can have a better online experience.

Some of the most common issues include Peer Pressure, Anxiety, Stress and Depression.

Peer Pressure

Peer pressure means feeling like you have to do something because people around you want you to or expect you to. It might be to make someone else happy or to fit in with a new group. Negative peer pressure is often related to influencing bullying behaviors, drinking alcohol, drug use and body image, all of which are harmful to wellbeing. The effects of such behaviors can decrease self-confidence, self-worth and distancing from family members and friends.

Symptoms
  • Change in Behavior : Sudden changes in habits or behavior, such as dressing differently, using new slang, or adopting new interests to fit in with a group.
  • Reluctance to Engage in Usual Activities : Avoiding activities they once enjoyed because they're not considered "cool" by their peers.
  • Mood Swings : Increased moodiness, irritability, or anxiety, especially after interacting with certain friends or peer groups.
  • Secretiveness : Becoming more secretive about their activities, whereabouts, and social interactions.
  • Resistance to Authority : Increased defiance or opposition to parents, teachers, or other authority figures due to influences from peers.
  • Uncharacteristic Risk-Taking : Engaging in risky behaviors they normally wouldn't, such as skipping school, experimenting with substances, or breaking rules.

How to Manage
  • Be Assertive : Steer clear of situations where you know you might be pressured into something you’re uncomfortable with.
  • Choose Friends Wisely : Surround yourself with friends who respect your decisions and support your values.
  • Know Your Values and Beliefs : Have a clear understanding of what's important to you, and stand firm on those principles.
  • Practice Saying No : Role-play scenarios where you might face peer pressure to make it easier to say "no" when the time comes.
  • Seek Support : Talk to someone you trust, like a family member, teacher, or counselor, about the pressures you're facing.
  • Avoid Tempting Situations : Steer clear of situations where you know you might be pressured into something you’re uncomfortable with.

Anxiety

Anxiety is what we feel when we are worried, tense or afraid – particularly about things that are about to happen, or which we think could happen in the future. Anxiety is a natural human response when we feel that we are under threat. It can be experienced through our thoughts, feelings and physical sensations.

Symptoms
  • Excessive Worry : Feeling worried or afraid about everyday situations more than normal.
  • Restlessness : Finding it hard to stay still or relax.
  • Fatigue : Feeling tired all the time, even if you haven't done much.
  • Difficulty Concentrating : Struggling to focus or having a hard time thinking clearly.
  • Irritability : Getting annoyed or frustrated more easily than usual.
  • Physical Symptoms : Experiencing things like muscle tension, headaches, a fast heartbeat, sweating, or stomach problems.

How to Manage
  • Practice Mindfulness and Meditation : Engaging in mindfulness exercises and meditation can help calm your mind and improve your ability to manage stress. Apps like Headspace and Calm offer guided sessions
  • Stay Physically Active : Regular exercise can reduce anxiety and improve your mood. Activities like walking, jogging, yoga, or even dancing can be beneficial.
  • Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle : Eating a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, and avoiding excessive caffeine and alcohol can have a positive impact on your anxiety levels.
  • Practice Deep Breathing : Techniques like deep breathing, progressive muscle relaxation, or guided imagery can help you relax and reduce anxiety symptoms.
  • Limit Social Media and News Consumption : Taking breaks from social media and limiting exposure to distressing news can help reduce anxiety.
  • Seek Professional Help : Talking to a therapist or counselor can provide you with tools and techniques to manage anxiety effectively. Cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) is a common approach that can be particularly helpful.

Stress

Stress can be defined as a state of worry or mental tension caused by a difficult situation. Stress is a natural human response that prompts us to address challenges and threats in our lives. Everyone experiences stress to some degree.

Symptoms
  • Headaches : Frequent tension headaches or migraines.
  • Muscle Tension : Tightness or pain in muscles, often in the neck, shoulders, or back.
  • Sleep Problems : Difficulty falling asleep, staying asleep, or experiencing restful sleep
  • Fatigue : Feeling constantly tired or drained of energy.
  • Irritability or Mood Swings : Increased frustration, anger, or feeling overwhelmed.
  • Digestive Issues : Stomachaches, indigestion, or changes in appetite.

How to Manage
  • Exercise Regularly : Physical activity can help reduce stress hormones and boost endorphins, the body's natural mood lifters. Even a short daily walk can make a difference.
  • Practice Relaxation Techniques : Techniques such as deep breathing, meditation, or progressive muscle relaxation can help calm the mind and reduce stress.
  • Maintain a Healthy Lifestyle : Eating a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, and avoiding excessive caffeine and alcohol can help manage stress levels.
  • Stay Organized : Planning your time and breaking tasks into manageable steps can reduce feelings of being overwhelmed and help you stay on top of your responsibilities.
  • Connect with Others : Spending time with friends and family, or talking to a trusted person about your stress, can provide emotional support and help you feel less isolated.
  • Engage in Hobbies : Taking time to do activities you enjoy can be a great way to relax and distract yourself from stress. Whether it's reading, gardening, painting, or playing a sport, engaging in hobbies can provide a much-needed break.

Depression

Depressive disorder (also known as depression) is a common mental disorder. It involves a depressed mood or loss of pleasure or interest in activities for long periods of time. Depression is different from regular mood changes and feelings about everyday life.

Symptoms
  • Persistent Sadness : Feeling sad, empty, or hopeless most of the time.
  • Loss of Interest : Losing interest or pleasure in activities that were once enjoyable.
  • Changes in Appetite or Weight : Significant weight loss or gain, or changes in appetite.
  • Sleep Disturbances : Trouble sleeping, waking up too early, or oversleeping.
  • Fatigue and Low Energy : eeling tired and lacking energy almost every day.
  • Difficulty Concentrating : Trouble focusing, remembering things, or making decisions.

How to Manage
  • Therapy : Engaging in talk therapy, such as cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) or psychotherapy, can provide a safe space to explore your feelings and develop coping strategies.
  • Medication : Antidepressant medications can help balance chemicals in the brain that affect mood. It's important to work with a healthcare provider to find the right medication and dosage.x
  • Exercise : Regular physical activity can boost your mood by increasing endorphins, the body's natural mood lifters. Even short daily walks can make a difference.
  • Healthy Lifestyle : Eating a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, and avoiding alcohol and drugs can help improve overall well-being and manage symptoms of depression.
  • Social Support : Connecting with friends, family, or support groups can provide emotional support and reduce feelings of isolation. Sharing your experiences with others who understand can be very comforting.
  • Mindfulness and Relaxation Techniques : Practices like meditation, deep breathing exercises, and yoga can help reduce stress and promote a sense of calm and well-being.
