Peer Pressure

Peer pressure means feeling like you have to do something because people around you want you to or expect you to. It might be to make someone else happy or to fit in with a new group. Negative peer pressure is often related to influencing bullying behaviors, drinking alcohol, drug use and body image, all of which are harmful to wellbeing. The effects of such behaviors can decrease self-confidence, self-worth and distancing from family members and friends.

Symptoms
  • Change in Behavior : Sudden changes in habits or behavior, such as dressing differently, using new slang, or adopting new interests to fit in with a group.
  • Reluctance to Engage in Usual Activities : Avoiding activities they once enjoyed because they're not considered "cool" by their peers.
  • Mood Swings : Increased moodiness, irritability, or anxiety, especially after interacting with certain friends or peer groups.
  • Secretiveness : Becoming more secretive about their activities, whereabouts, and social interactions.
  • Resistance to Authority : Increased defiance or opposition to parents, teachers, or other authority figures due to influences from peers.
  • Uncharacteristic Risk-Taking : Engaging in risky behaviors they normally wouldn't, such as skipping school, experimenting with substances, or breaking rules.

How to manage
  • Be Assertive : Steer clear of situations where you know you might be pressured into something you’re uncomfortable with.
  • Choose Friends Wisely : Surround yourself with friends who respect your decisions and support your values.
  • Know Your Values and Beliefs : Have a clear understanding of what's important to you, and stand firm on those principles.
  • Practice Saying No : Role-play scenarios where you might face peer pressure to make it easier to say "no" when the time comes.
  • Seek Support : Talk to someone you trust, like a family member, teacher, or counselor, about the pressures you're facing.
  • Avoid Tempting Situations : Steer clear of situations where you know you might be pressured into something you’re uncomfortable with.
